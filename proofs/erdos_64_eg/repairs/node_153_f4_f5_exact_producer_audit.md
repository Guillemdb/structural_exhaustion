This audit records the paper-faithful F4/F5 ownership boundary after the
stage-major restricted-prefix construction.  It does not modify the live
manuscript and does not mark node [153] green.

## F5 reusable operation

`Core.FiniteExactStateCorridor.Profile` scans the actual stored prefix list.
If its length is at most the exact state-cardinality `Q`, it returns the
terminal case.  Otherwise it inspects only the first `Q+1` stages and returns
two strictly ordered occurrences with equal exact codes.  Its repeat payload
proves:

- both occurrences come from the supplied stage list;
- the second occurrence and the intervening index span are bounded by `Q`;
- their cached stage values retain exact inspected-list occurrence proofs and
  membership in the supplied list; and
- only their supplied structural codes are equal.  The Core profile has no
  response field or response-reflection theorem; exact target comparison is
  the subsequent F2 operation.

Thus the finite pigeonhole and bounded-index parts of F5 are implemented
without enumerating any ambient graph, context, path, or state universe.
`Graph.WalkPrefixBoundedExchange.repeatedSupport` then cuts the corresponding
contiguous sublist from the one stored path support, and proves that its
cardinality is at most `Q+1`.  It does not search for a path or support.

For the restricted cold corridor, the two boundary roles are read literally
as the fixed anchor outside endpoint and the moving prefix endpoint.  Their
half-edge identities are the corresponding fixed carrier roles, while the
two offset fields are the anchor-window and cyclic-successor-window offsets.
In particular the moving boundary degree is not replaced by the terminal
successor degree.  The stage schedule drops position zero before running the
corridor profile.  Every stored stage therefore has positive prefix length,
and a repeated pair directly supplies two genuine two-boundary pieces; no
degenerate-boundary outcome is added to the manuscript flow.
