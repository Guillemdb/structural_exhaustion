# Node [144]: attachment-mismatch contract

## Exact input

The attachment scan runs on the fixed universe

`WindowIndex × (Fin 13 × (Bool × PortRole))`.

Its first mismatch therefore supplies exactly:

- one selected packed window and one of its thirteen literal positions;
- a Boolean choosing the first or second slot of each collided scheduled pair;
- one of the three port roles (left shoulder, right shoulder, or buffer);
- prefix agreement on every earlier coordinate; and
- failure of equivalence between the two literal adjacency predicates.

Because adjacency is decidable, the failure has exactly two orientations:
the window vertex is adjacent only to the first role vertex, or only to the
second role vertex.  In either case the two role vertices are distinct.

## Token-class refinement

The homogeneous audit retains the exact class of the common capacity token:

- window incidence;
- remainder surplus; or
- primitive carrier.

The same local mismatch coordinate is retained in all three cases.  The class
proof records the accounting origin of the overloaded fibre; it does not
change an adjacency mismatch into a target-response mismatch.

target-complete compression, or delocalization only after two **declared
response coordinates** with compatible bounded support and boundary-degree
data have been constructed.  The present Lean collision stores scheduled

construct a boundaried piece or a target-response coordinate for this first

Consequently the dependency-ready output is an unmatched-coordinate residual
containing:

- the proof-carrying first hit and prefix exactness;
- exact window/position/side/role projections;
- the exclusive literal adjacency orientation;
- distinctness of the two role vertices; and
- the exact window/remainder/primitive token-class route.

No literal cycle follows from a single asymmetric incidence.  A cycle would
require an additional return path or chord, neither of which is supplied by
the mismatch row.  Likewise, row inequality alone does not prove a
target-defective quotient.

response coordinates and prove that this exact mismatch either reflects a
