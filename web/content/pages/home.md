# Hypostructure

Hypostructure turns a mathematical argument into a typed proof program. A problem author registers irreducible semantics; the framework owns finite decisions, accumulated evidence, routing, and closure.

## The proof pipeline

A proof starts from one stable residual. Each CT reads the literal predecessor, computes one typed outcome, and extends the accumulated proof ledger. Framework-owned routes preserve the complete ledger while moving to the next CT.

## What authors provide

Authors provide the problem, target, domain semantics, and the local mathematical facts that cannot be derived generically. `Core.Provision` records whether a dependency is authored, inferred, or produced by the framework.

## What the framework provides

Core provides residuals, queries, decisions, focus, joins, coordinates, assembly, budgets, execution, routing, and closure. The Graph and PDE layers specialize those contracts without creating separate proof engines.
